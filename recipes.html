Here's my recipe for pancakes. And when I say pancakes, I mean pan CAKES. These are some thicc boys.

Ingredients
  • Dry Ingredients
    • 1 cup all purpose flour
    • 1.5 tsp baking powder
    • 0.5 tsp baking soda
    • Pinch salt
  • Wet Ingredients
    • 3 tbsp sugar
    • 1 cup buttermilk *
    • 1 egg
    • 2 tbsp melted butter **
    • 0.5 tsp vanilla extract

* Buttermilk can be made at home by thoroughly mixing equal parts yogurt and water

** Can be substituted for coconut oil to add subtle coconut flavor if desired

Instructions
  1. Sift and combine dry ingredients in large bowl.
  2. Separate egg whites and yolk into separate bowls.
  3. Whisk egg whites until they form soft peaks. It is not necessary to go quite this far, but it does make a difference.
  4. Stir sugar into yolks until fully combined and color lightens slightly.
  5. Add butter to yolk mixture and stir until homogenous.
  6. Stream buttermilk into yolk, whisking constantly to combine.
  7. Gently fold egg whites with a spatula into buttermilk mixture, taking care not to deflate the whites.
  8. Add wet mixture gently into dry ingredients, fold together until just barely combined.
  9. Chill batter in fridge for 30-60 minutes.
  10. Ladle batter into a pan lubricated with butter over medium heat. Do not disturb it after pouring.
  11. Add whatever other ingredients you want to flavor the pancake (blueberries, chocolate chips, cooked bacon, etc.
  12. Flip when bubbles begin to pop in the middle of the pancake.
  13. Cook on the other side for 30-60 seconds until no raw batter is visible.

Ingredients
  • 3 thick slices brioche *
  • 2 eggs
  • 0.5 cups whole milk
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 0.5 tsp cinnamon
  • Pinch salt
  • Powdered Sugar

* Challah can also be used here. Other breads can work, but are not as good

Instructions
  1. Leave bread on wire rack overnight to let it become stale.
  2. Crack eggs into casserole dish or any dish deep enough to hold the dipping liquid but wide enough to fit a slice of the bread.
  3. Whisk cinnamon, sugar, and salt until fully and evenly combined.
  4. Slowly stream in milk and continue whisking.
  5. Soak a slice of stale bread in dipping liquid until saturated. This should take no more than a couple of minutes.
  6. Flip bread to soak other side and warm up a skillet over medium heat.
  7. Once pan is heated, grease it with butter and toast the soaked bread.
  8. While cooking one slice, place the next slice into the liquid.
  9. Once browned on bottom, flip toast and cook on the other side until it's browned as well.
  10. Serve on a plate with a generous dusting of powdered sugar. Using a sifter ensures even, gentle coverage
  11. Top with anything you want: fresh berries, maple syrup, honey, nutella, pistachios, sour cream, guacamole, you name it.
